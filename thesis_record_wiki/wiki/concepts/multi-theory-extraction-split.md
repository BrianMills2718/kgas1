# Summary

The multi-theory extraction split is the design choice between representing one paper as several related theories/metatheories or as one primary theory. The Turner/Oakes artifacts preserve both paths: a six-theory nested extraction and a single Self-Categorization Theory extraction.

# Why It Matters

The two outputs optimize for different uses:

- multi-theory extraction preserves theoretical landscape, metatheory, dependencies, and derivative theories
- single-theory extraction produces a cleaner object for execution or downstream application

Neither is universally correct. The extraction mode should be selected from the user's analysis goal and the paper's actual theory structure.
