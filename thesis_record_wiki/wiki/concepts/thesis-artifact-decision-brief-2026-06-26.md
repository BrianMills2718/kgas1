# Recommendation

Default to **two separate artifacts**:

1. **Private technical archive:** keep `phd_thesis_work` and the thesis wiki as the full provenance record.
2. **Derived public/portfolio narrative:** later create a smaller reviewed story about KGAS, theory-aware GraphRAG, schema extraction, and evidence discipline.

Do not make the raw thesis repository itself the public artifact. Do not treat dissertation restart as the default path unless Brian explicitly wants to re-enter an academic program or produce a dissertation-shaped manuscript.

# Why This Is The Best Default

The recovered work is valuable, but it is not one clean product. It is a research lineage with:

- strong historical provenance;
- a serious dissertation claim surface;
- bounded current runtime proof;
- sensitive raw archives;
- proposal/HSPC caveats;
- old ambitions that remain intellectually useful but are not all current claims.

That shape argues for separation. The private archive should stay complete and messy. The public artifact should be derived, smaller, reviewed, and honest about what was proven versus designed.

# Option Map

| Option | Fit | Upside | Cost/Risk | Recommendation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Private technical archive | Excellent | Preserves full history, provenance, code, wiki, and decision record. | Not easily legible to outsiders. | Keep as canonical internal source. |
| Public/portfolio narrative | Strong | Communicates the intellectual contribution without exposing raw archives. | Needs privacy/narrative review. | Best next outward-facing artifact. |
| Dissertation restart | Possible | The claim map and validation menu make restart feasible. | Requires major human commitment, fresh validation design, and governance review. | Only if Brian wants that life path again. |
| Current productization | Weak-to-moderate | Runtime slices exist and could be hardened. | Risks confusing recovered proof with product readiness. | Defer unless implementation becomes the goal. |

# Recommended Public Story

If Brian wants a public narrative, the strongest honest frame is:

> KGAS was an attempt to make social-science theory operational inside a GraphRAG-style analysis system: extracting theories into schemas, applying them to discourse, comparing graph/table/vector representations, and preserving evidence/uncertainty discipline.

Avoid:

- "finished AI research product";
- "validated dissertation system";
- "lossless theory automation";
- "public-ready archive";
- "HSPC-approved study."

# Next Safe Agent Work

If Brian gives no further direction, the safest next agent task is:

1. draft a **private public-narrative outline** inside the repo;
2. source every claim to wiki pages;
3. exclude raw archive paths, tweet text, credentials, and detailed security-risk summaries;
4. do not publish anything;
5. mark it explicitly as a draft for Brian review.

# Human Decisions Needed

Brian should decide:

1. Is the next artifact mainly for **self/recovery**, **public portfolio**, **academic restart**, or **implementation revival**?
2. Should public readers see the security/privacy history, or should that remain private?
3. Should the public narrative mention leaving the thesis program, or only describe the research lineage?
4. Is KGAS presented as a system, a method, a research program, or a case study in evidence discipline?
