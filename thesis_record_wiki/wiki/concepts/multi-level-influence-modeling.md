# Summary

Multi-level influence modeling is the pattern where a theory links influence activity across individual cognition, group dynamics, leadership coalitions, mass publics, messages, channels, strategies, objectives, and assessment metrics.

# Why It Matters

This is one place where property-graph representation is substantively justified. The influence-operations framework is not a single sequence, matrix, hierarchy, or statistical result. It is a campaign-planning model with many actors, objectives, actions, audience segments, message attributes, outcome measures, and feedback loops.

The important caution is scope: this supports property-graph modeling for multi-level influence frameworks, not property-graph-by-default for every theory.
