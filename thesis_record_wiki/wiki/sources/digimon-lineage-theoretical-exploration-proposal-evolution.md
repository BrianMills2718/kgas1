# Critique And Response

The Prateek critique asks for stronger comparisons to out-of-the-box GraphRAG and standard RAG, more dataset-structure detail, explicit filtering/cleaning, clearer evaluation strategy, Google Concordia and Park-style LLM-ABM references, ABM structure and calibration detail, and acknowledgment that offline behavior may drive attitude shifts outside KGAS visibility. [5]

Later summaries claim these questions were addressed in the final proposal: GraphRAG comparison, dataset structure, data filtering, evaluation strategy, Concordia reference, ABM structure and calibration, and offline-behavior limitations all receive mapped proposal locations. [6]

The proof-of-concept tone note says most claim-softening principles were already implemented: no predetermined performance targets, future-tense "will demonstrate" framing, no effect-size promises, explicit limitations, and an uncertainty-propagation example. It recommends only minor additions around process metrics and infrastructure framing. [7]

# Proposal Compression And Framing

The final submission summary describes a 485-line, approximately 20-page main proposal plus five annexes. It says the rewrite achieved a 19% text reduction, standardized terminology, added GraphRAG/standard RAG comparison, made the Kunst COVID conspiracy dataset prominent, and framed KGAS as proof-of-concept infrastructure rather than already-proven functionality. [6]

The final revisions summary records specific claim-discipline edits: replacing "lossless" with "information-preserving conversions between formats within theoretical constructs of interest," changing RQ wording from "improve" to "characterize improvements," adding ABM parameter ranges, documenting rather than optimizing alignment, and defining operational indicators for insight. [8]

These edits are valuable thesis-history evidence because they show active correction of overclaim risk rather than only polished final claims.

# Older Versus Later Drafts

The older 47-page proposal already positioned KGAS relative to RAG, GraphRAG, DIGIMON, and StructGPT. It framed KGAS as theory-driven graph construction, hybrid GraphRAG, and an investigative workbench, while acknowledging the bounded nature of online-discourse modeling and the offline-behavior risk. [9]

The later compressed drafts sharpened the dissertation form around three essays, the Kunst dataset, DEPI x Level x Component framing, a theory meta-schema, cross-modal graph/table/vector analysis, validation baselines, and agent-based modeling parameterized from observed psychological and network data. [10]

The major evolution is not a complete change in idea. It is a shift from expansive system vision toward submission-ready proof-of-concept framing: more concise, more bounded, more explicit about limitations, and more careful about not promising performance before validation.

# Citation Choices
