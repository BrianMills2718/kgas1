# Analysis Philosophy

The strongest methodological contribution in this slice is the distinction between analyzing text as an object and using text as evidence about the world. The `ANALYTIC_TIER_MULTIPLE_MEANINGS.md` note shows that descriptive, explanatory, predictive, and prescriptive tiers mean different things depending on whether the target is text-internal or text-external. [3]

The systematic framework broadens this into a multidimensional taxonomy: object of analysis, type of claim, temporal orientation, and level of inference. It argues that asking "what analytic tier is this theory?" is less useful than asking what questions the theory answers, what claims it can support, what textual evidence it uses, and what outside knowledge it requires. [4]

This is important lineage evidence because it explains why later KGAS work moved away from a single graph-extraction framing toward model-form routing, theory profiles, and explicit claim discipline.

# Two-Stage Analysis

The two-stage critique tests a process where Stage 1 creates a comprehensive Social Identity Theory structure and Stage 2 applies an analytic question to that structure. It reports genuine value: delayed evaluation captured Carter's diplomatic identity work that direct single-stage analysis had filtered out as a poor standard SIT fit. [5]

The same critique also preserves limitations. Stage 1 still embeds theoretical judgment, Stage 2 quality remains decisive, comprehensive structuring may be inefficient for simple questions, and a perfect separation between structuring and evaluation may be impossible. [5]

This maps directly onto later wiki concepts about evidence-claim discipline: the method is promising, but the artifact itself is exploratory method design, not system-level proof.

# Architecture Exploration

The six-level automation note proposes routing theory components into specialized implementation families: formulas, algorithms, procedures, rules, sequences, and frameworks. It says Level 1 formulas were implemented while Levels 2-6 were architecture-defined or pending. [6]

The cross-modal orchestration plan is a three-phase implementation proposal for LLM-driven mode selection, graph-table-vector conversion, workflow optimization, and integrity validation. It lists missing pieces explicitly: mode-selection intelligence, comprehensive conversion, orchestration logic, and round-trip validation. [7]

Together these notes show the bridge from "extract a theory schema" to "use the schema to choose or generate computational behavior." They should be treated as target-design lineage, not as verified active capabilities.

# Implementation Claims

The theory-to-code workflow describes a two-layer system: theory extraction into a V12 schema, followed by computational implementation across formulas, algorithms, procedures, rules, sequences, and frameworks. It includes code sketches for formula generation, OWL/SWRL rules, state machines, and classifiers. [8]

The enhanced-tools integration note claims memory-aware entity extraction, reasoning-guided relationship discovery, collaborative graph building, and message-bus insight sharing. The claims include estimated benefits such as accuracy and false-positive improvements, but this slice does not itself provide benchmark evidence for those numbers. [9]

The Social Identity Theory example is valuable as a worked design artifact. It shows construct definitions, observable indicators, relationship conditions, confidence fields, boundary ambiguity, and entity-resolution concerns in a congressional-hearing-style analysis. It is explicitly framed as a complete example, not as a current-code verification result. [10]

# Interpretation

This slice preserves the intellectual turning point where the project was trying to decide what kind of analysis KGAS should support: text properties, communicative effects, world inferences, theory automation, or all of them. The best durable lesson is not any one architecture proposal; it is the need to specify the analysis object and claim type before designing schemas or tools.

Use this page to reconstruct thesis evolution and method-design rationale. Do not treat any implementation claim here as current system status without a separate evidence or runtime-verification page.
