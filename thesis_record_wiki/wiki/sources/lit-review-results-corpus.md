# WorldView Application Attempts

`worldview_application_final_assessment.md` compares three WorldView/Carter application attempts:

| Attempt | Context passed | Reported fidelity |
|---|---|---:|
| partial schema | vocabulary only | about 25% |
| focused purpose | vocabulary plus purpose context | about 70% |
| complete schema | classification, process, telos, ontology, analytics | about 85% |

The assessment concludes that process steps, purpose context, classification, and ontology are essential for useful application. [2]

`enhanced_extraction_final_report.md` reports a fourth enhanced attempt with operational examples and extraction-density expectations. It claims 65 statements, three SALT references, compound statements, and about 95% fidelity. [3]

# Cognitive Mapping Critiques

The cognitive-mapping critiques preserve a critical distinction:

- iterative/multi-pass extraction improved content completeness
- structural compliance with the intended meta-schema still failed [4]

`iterative_extraction_assessment.md` says iteration improved completeness from about 85% to 98%, including missing truth values. But it also says the generated structure drifted away from `meta_schema_8`. [1]

This is one of the clearest results-corpus examples of why valid-looking content is not enough: schema shape is part of the contract.

# Simplified Meta-Schema Findings

`schema_vs_paper_critique.md` and `simplified_meta_schema_comparison.md` argue that the simplified four-component meta-schema improved theory fidelity for Semantic Hypergraph and Young 1996. The simplified approach captured Semantic Hypergraph type codes and inference rules better than earlier attempts and represented Young 1996 more naturally as a network/cognitive-map structure. [5]

But the same critique says both schemas extracted frameworks better than worked applications: Semantic Hypergraph needed parsing examples and pattern libraries; Young needed Carter's actual 48-concept cognitive-map instance and quantitative results. [5]

# Interpretation

The results corpus is not a polished benchmark suite. It is a record of learning:

- complete schema context matters
- operational examples matter
- extraction density expectations matter
- iterative validation can improve completeness
- structural validation must be separate from content validation
- theory framework extraction and theory application/instantiation are different tasks
