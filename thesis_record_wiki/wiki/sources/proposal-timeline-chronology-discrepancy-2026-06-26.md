# Summary

The proposal timeline evidence is internally inconsistent. The safest preservation move is to record the variants rather than select one.

There are at least four timeline claims:

| Source | Timeline claim | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Older 47-page proposal | Target completion February 2026; proposal/theory July-September 2025; integration/finalization January-February 2026. [1] | Focused proof-of-concept schedule. |
| Full August draft main text | "12 months" from September 2025 proposal approval through February 2026. [2] | Numerically inconsistent: September 2025 to February 2026 is not 12 months. |
| Full August draft embedded Annex D | "6 months" from August 2025 proposal approval through February 2026. [2] | More consistent with August-February compressed schedule, but conflicts with "12 months" wording. |
| Final rewrite main text | "18 months" from September 2025 proposal approval through February 2027. [3] | Aligns with standalone Annex E. |
| Standalone Annex E | 18 months from September 2025 through February 2027, with August 6 2025 submission and August 29 2025 proposal-defense target. [4] | Most detailed schedule artifact. |

# What Changed

The old and full August drafts preserve an aggressive completion path ending in February 2026. [1][2]

The final rewrite and standalone Annex E preserve a longer path ending in February 2027. [3][4]

The submission summary says timeline content was "properly moved to Annex E" and that the final package was ready for submission on August 6, 2025. [5] It does not resolve whether the intended defense date was February 2026 or February 2027.

# Interpretation

The strongest reading is that the timeline was revised during final proposal packaging, but not all intermediate wording was internally coherent:

- the compressed proposal needed a short schedule section;
- detailed scheduling was moved to an annex;
- some older "February 2026" language persisted in pre-final or full-draft material;
- the final rewrite and Annex E agree on February 2027.

This does not prove the actual program schedule. It only proves what the preserved proposal artifacts claimed at different points.

# Preservation Rule

Do not normalize these dates in future summaries. Use source-qualified language:

- "Older proposal versions targeted February 2026."
- "The final rewrite and Annex E targeted February 2027."
- "The full August draft contains internally inconsistent 6-month/12-month language."
- "The final submission summary confirms Annex E was the intended detailed timeline surface, but does not itself adjudicate the date conflict."

# Why This Matters

The timeline discrepancy is a small but useful example of why newer docs are not automatically cleaner. A later final package can improve claim discipline while still carrying schedule ambiguity.

For reconstructing Brian's thesis work, the important fact is not just the final date. It is the evolution from an aggressive proof-of-concept schedule toward a longer essay-by-essay development, validation, advanced-analysis, integration, and defense plan.
